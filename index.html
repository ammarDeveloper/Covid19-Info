    #What is the meaning of COVID-19?

    COVID-19 is a disease caused by a new strain of coronavirus. 'CO' stands for corona, 'VI' for virus, and 'D' for disease. Formerly, this disease was referred to as '2019 novel coronavirus' or '2019-nCoV.'

    Do's:

    • Maintaining proper hygiene and washing hands with an alcohol-based sanitizer.
    • Cover your face with a mask while going out.
    • Covering mouth with a tissue paper or your elbow when coughing or sneezing.
    • Approaching a doctor if you notice any symptoms that you are affected.
    • Drink plenty of liquids and eat nutritious food.

    Dont's:

    • Going to crowded places and cancel all non-essential travel plans.
    • Touching your eyes, nose or mouth with unwashed hands.
    • Standing too close to other people when you are outside.
    • Eating undercooked or raw meat from any source.
    • Touching surfaces usually used by the public like railings, doors, gates, etc.

    #Symptoms:

    COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people will develop mild to moderate illness and recover without hospitalization.

    Most common symptoms:

    • fever
    • dry cough
    • tiredness

    Less common symptoms:

    • aches and pains
    • sore throat
    • diarrhoea
    • conjunctivitis
    • headache
    • loss of taste or smell
    • a rash on skin, or discolouration of fingers or toes

    Serious common symptoms:

    • difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
    • chest pain or pressure
    • loss of speech or movement

    Seek immediate medical attention if you have serious symptoms. Always call before visiting your doctor or health facility.

    People with mild symptoms who are otherwise healthy should manage their symptoms at home.

    On average it takes 5–6 days from when someone is infected with the virus for symptoms to show, however it can take up to 14 days.

    #FAQ's:

    Q:Are there any side effects from the COVID-19 vaccine?

    A: Like any vaccine, COVID-19 vaccines can cause mild, short term side effects, such as a low-grade fever or pain or redness at the injection site. Most reactions to vaccines are mild and go away within a few days on their own. More serious or long-lasting side effects to vaccines are possible but extremely rare.

    Q:Can I get COVID-19 while swimming?

    A:The COVID-19 virus does not transmit through water while swimming. However, the virus spreads between people when someone has close contact with an infected person.

    Q:Are tobacco users have a higher risk of being infected with COVID-19?

    A: Tobacco users have a higher risk of being infected with the virus through the mouth while smoking cigarettes or using other tobacco products. If smokers contract the COVID-19 virus, they face a greater risk of getting a severe infection as their lung health is already compromised.

    Q:Can I reschedule my COVID-19 vaccination appointment?

    A:The appointment can be rescheduled at any time. In case you do not go for vaccination on the date of appointment, you can reschedule the appointment by clicking on “Reschedule” tab

    Q:What are the mild-to-moderate side effects of the COVID-19 vaccine?

    A:Mild-to-moderate side effects, like a low-grade fever or muscle aches, are normal and not a cause for alarm: they are signs that the body's immune system is responding to the vaccine, specifically the antigen (a substance that triggers an immune response), and is gearing up to fight the virus.

    Q:What are the common side effects of the Covaxin® COVID-19 vaccine?

    A: Covaxin®: Some mild symptoms AEFIs may occur like injection site pain, headache, fatigue, fever, body ache, abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, dizziness-giddiness, tremor, sweating, cold, cough and injection site swelling. No other vaccine-related serious adverse effects have been reported.

    Q:How long does it take to develop an adequate immune response after receiving the COVID-19 vaccine?

    A: Adequate immune response takes 2-3 weeks after completion of entire vaccination schedule i.e., after the second dose of COVISHIELD® and COVAXIN®.

    Q:Should you avoid alcohol after receiving the COVID-19 vaccine?

    A: As per experts, there is no evidence of alcohol impairing the effectiveness of the vaccine.

    Q:Can the COVID-19 survive in drinking water?

    A: Currently, there is no evidence about the survival of the COVID-19 virus in drinking-water or sewage.
